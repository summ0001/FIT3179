Sea turtles are ancient marine reptiles that have been navigating the oceans for over 100 million years. They play a crucial role in maintaining the health of marine ecosystems, from controlling jellyfish populations to supporting seagrass beds that help sustain coastal habitats. Despite their ecological importance, many species are threatened by habitat loss, pollution, climate change, and illegal hunting. This interactive visualisation allows you to explore various aspects of marine turtles, including their anatomy, diet, threats, distribution, and the global conservation efforts that help protect them.
